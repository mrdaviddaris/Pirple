I grew up eating pancakes. I know pancakes are a favorite American breakfast - aw, heck - lunch and dinner too! Myself, I would eat them all the time if I wasn't worried about my waistline. I'm talking about pancakes with butter and syrup...maple syrup. And, where is the best maple syrup from? New England! Primarily Vermont and Maine. Disbelievers may scoff and those from the Pacific Northwest loudest, but I refuse to listen to reason - the trait of a true Nor'Easter!

As a Dad, I know how serious it is to get my hungry monsters fed on Saturday morning. How often have we woken up to find no milk or eggs? Too many times to count. Therefore, this recipe will accept substitutions for milk and egg. Dads, I understand your pain. No need to look up alternative recipes. This one has all the flexibility you need!

Dave's True New England Pancakes 🥞:

Ingredients 🥣:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 Tbsp oil
  • 1 cup water

Directions ðŸ§­:

Combine all dry ingredients in a bowl. Combine all wet ingredients in another bowl. Combine weight into dry ingredients. Mix until smooth. Heat griddle or frying pan to medium heat. Use a little oil in the pan. Pour batter into middle of pan. When edges are slightly dry and bubbles have formed on top of pancake, flip. At this point, I add a slice of butter because it keeps My Precious moist. Transfer to plate. Add your choice of topping. Mine? Of course: pure Vermony maple syrup.

While waiting for your pancakes to cook enjoy a page of pancake quotes starting with this one:

"There is hardship in everything except eating pancakes." -- Charles Spurgeon

Enjoy!
